OpenAI is an AI research organization founded by Elon Musk in January 2016 to explore, develop, and deploy technology for the benefit of humanity. It‚Äôs headquartered in San Francisco, with headquarters in Mountain View, California.

And GPT-2?

OpenAI recently unveiled a language model called GPT-2 that, given some input text, predicts the coming sentences. GPT-2 also supports the idea of the ‚ÄúGrammar of Reasoning‚Äù (GRR), in which the model attempts to extract sentences that would make the most intuitive sense, in terms of human understanding. For example, if you input ‚Äúthe dog ate the cat‚Äù and the model predicts ‚Äúdogs eat cats‚Äù, the GRR system would make that sentence as the most probable result. The problem is that even though GRR is very good at answering the question, its predictions are still highly contingent. For example, it is still possible to be a cat-eating dog, and then someone else who doesn‚Äôt eat cats can still eat your dog, but the GRR system would not make such a mistake for the second sentence.
